Terminology

ANIME A style of Japanese film and television animation, typically aimed at adults as well as children.
OVA Original Video Animation. Animated films and series, made specifically for home release without prior showings on television or in theatres.
ONA Original Net Animation. Animated films and series, made specifically for internet release without prior showings on television or in theatres.
SPECIAL Specials are usually stand alone episodes from a TV show. They might feature popular characters from other shows in collaboration. They might also be shorter or longer than a regular episode.
DEMENTIA The Dementia tag is usually given to shows that try to toy with your mind by incorporating the metaphysical and the abstract.
DEMONS Demons can encompass anything from literal demon characters to monsters, beasts, ghosts and other demon-kind. Often a sub-genre of Horror but also blends well with other genre.
ECCHI Ecchi is a genre generally accepted as being full of slight sexual scenes (mild enough for a general audience) and scenarios derived form sexual innuendos.
GAME Game anime revolve around the idea of gaming and playing. It could focus on a card game, a board game, a puzzle game as well as virtual reality and online games.
HAREM A male character, surrounded by multiple female characters gearing towards an awkward love situation. Harem is not always affiliated with romance and is sometimes just used for comic relief.
HENTAI Hentai translates to 'pervert' from Japanese and is a genre meant for mature audiences. This genre will usually focus on highly explicit content rather than storyline progression.
JOSEI Josei is more of a demographic but is considered a genre in anime. It specifically targets female viewers aged between 18-40. The focus is often on very realistic romance with less-idealized portrayals.
MECHA Mecha stands for mechanical and anime in this genre will always have robots or mechanical suits/objects in it.
PARODY A parody will have countless references of other anime showcased throughout the plot. It could be a popular running gag, a famous symbol, a character, a reference or any attempt to bring another show into it's domain.
SEINEN A demographic but also considered a genre. It targets males between 18-40. The content will often include much more explicit content like gore, sex and violence.
SHOUJO Shoujo is also a demographic genre. It targets girls with an age range of 10-18. These shows are usually idealized and well rounded and coupled with romance and comedy.
SHOUJO-AI Shoujo-ai means 'girl's love' and is about female characters (typically young girls) who show love to each other. Usually the romance is mild and portrays blooming feelings rather than intimate contact.
SHOUNEN Shonen is a demographic genre that targets boys aged 10-18. The shows are usually well rounded and idealized. Most of the time they become the most trending mainstream shows.
SHONEN-AI Shounen-ai translates to 'boy's love' and contains romance between male characters. Usually the romance is mild and portrays blooming feelings of love rather than intimate contact.
SLICE OF LIFE This genre portrays mundane good ol’ life. Stories depicted in this genre are realistically set in the domain of real life. Nothing out-of-the-blue happens, as it’s role is to portray everyday life in a realistic light as much as possible.
YAOI Yaoi is the much more sexually explicit counterpart to Shounen-ai (boy's love). This genre covers male to male relationships in a more mature light and unlike shounen-ai, will go all the way in both romance and intimacy.
YURI Yuri is the much more sexually explicit counterpart to Shoujo-ai (girls's love). This genre covers female to female relationships in a more mature light and unlike shoujo-ai, will go all the way in both romance and intimacy.
