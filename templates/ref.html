Terminology

ANIME A style of Japanese film and television animation, typically aimed at adults as well as children.
OVA Original Video Animation. Animated films and series, made specifically for home release without prior showings on television or in theatres.
ONA Original Net Animation. Animated films and series, made specifically for internet release without prior showings on television or in theatres.
SPECIAL Specials are usually stand alone episodes from a TV show. They might feature popular characters from other shows in collaboration. They might also be shorter or longer than a regular episode.
DEMENTIA The Dementia tag is usually given to shows that try to toy with your mind by incorporating the metaphysical and the abstract.
DEMONS Demons is more of a sub-genre and will most likely include demon characters such as literal demons, monsters, beasts and ghouls .
ECCHI Ecchi is a mildly sexual genre, full of innuendos intended for a general audience.
GAME Game tends to revolve around gaming and playing. From card games to board games as well as virtual reality and online games.
HAREM When several female characters have a love interest in the same male character. Harem is not always used for romance and instead, is sometimes just used for comic relief.
HENTAI Hentai meaning 'pervert' in Japanese is for mature audiences. It focuses more on it's explicit content rather than story line progression.
JOSEI Josei is the female 18-40 demographic, but is considered a genre in anime. The focus is often on very realistic romance.
MECHA Mecha stands for mechanical and this genre will almost always involve robots or people in mechanical suits.
SEINEN The 18-40 male demographic, also considered a genre in anime. The content is usually more explicit and will not shy away from gore, sex and violence.
SHOUJO Shoujo is the 10-18 female demographic. These shows are idealized and well rounded and often shown in conjunction with romance and comedy.
SHOUJO-AI Shoujo-ai means 'girl's love' and is about female characters (typically young girls) who share a mild romance with each other.
SHOUNEN Shonen is the 10-18 male demographic. The shows are usually well rounded and idealized. More often than not they become the most popular mainstream shows.
SHONEN-AI Shounen-ai translates to 'boy's love' and contains mild romance between male characters.

YAOI Yaoi is the more sexually explicit counterpart to Shounen-ai (boy's love). This genre covers male to male relationships and will go all the way in both romance and intimacy.
YURI Yuri is the much more sexually explicit counterpart to Shoujo-ai (girls's love). This genre covers female to female relationships and will not shy away from romance and intimacy.
